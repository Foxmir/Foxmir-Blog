Thanks to RL for bringing us together.

This is my personal blog. I write about RL-related theory, implementation, and reflections, including basic concepts, algorithm reproduction, training stability, experiment evaluation, result interpretation, as well as tools and side topics. 

At this stage, I’ll also use this space to document my path from RL fundamentals and practice toward MARL (Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning); over time, MARL-related topics will become a core focus.

Occasionally, I may also share other unrelated but interesting things.

I also keep a **Weekly Log** ( usually on Monday ) as a public progress record of my ongoing RL-related work. Each entry may include what I worked on, what was completed, what failed or changed, and what I plan to do next. It is not limited to finished results; it is meant to make the process, iterations, and direction of my work visible over time.

## Collaboration & Contact

I am currently open to a small number of RL-related collaboration inquiries, especially:

- RL project diagnosis and debugging review
- Experiment design and evaluation protocol review
- Reproduction review, training log analysis, and result interpretation
- Structured feedback for existing RL practice projects

It is a good fit if you already have code, configs, logs, training curves, experiment reports, or a clear problem description, and you want help with triage, review, and prioritizing the next debugging steps.

Currently not accepting: Python/framework basics, environment setup, from-scratch beginner Q&A, or real-time chat-style coaching.

If you would like to contact me, please briefly describe: the background of your problem, available materials, what you have already tried, and what kind of help you are looking for.
